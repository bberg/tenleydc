# Glover Park Timeline

## Colonial and Early Federal Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1770 | Beatty and Hawkins' Addition enlarges Georgetown by 200 acres; establishes northern extension boundary |
| 1808-1810 | First recorded settlements: Peter Colter (German immigrant) and Murray Barker (free Black man) establish farms in the village of Wilberforce along present-day Wisconsin Avenue |

## Cemetery and Poorhouse Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1832 | Holy Rood Cemetery established as Holy Trinity Catholic Church's parish burial ground |
| 1832 | Georgetown Poor and Work House established approximately at present Guy Mason Recreation Center location |
| 1833 | First burial at Holy Rood Cemetery: Jane Parks, age one, on April 20 |
| 1849-1855 | Georgetown University acquires four parcels that will become Holy Rood Cemetery; one plot deeded by William C. Corcoran |
| 1850-1870 | Holy Rood Cemetery enlarged and renamed (from Trinity Church Upper Grave Yard) in 1866 |
| 1875 | Georgetown Poor and Work House closes |

## Civil War and Butcher Syndicate Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1860s | Union Army maintains training camp and signal station on Glover Park heights during Civil War |
| 1846 | Charles Carroll Glover born in Macon County, North Carolina |
| Mid-1800s | Master butchers Joseph Weaver, Jacob H. Kengla, and Benjamin F. Hunt accumulate land that will become Glover Park |
| 1866 | Charles Carroll Glover starts as teller at Riggs & Company |
| Post-Civil War | Butcher syndicate's cattle market, stock pens, and slaughterhouses become increasingly irrelevant as livestock arrives by rail on other side of city |

## Streetcar Development and Early Residential Growth

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| c. 1890 | Georgetown & Tenallytown Street Railway begins operating along Wisconsin Avenue |
| 1896 | Charles Carroll Glover becomes President of Riggs National Bank |
| 1898 | Industrial Home School (serving homeless newsboys) established on former poorhouse site |
| 1902 | Industrial Home School constructs schoolhouse building (now Guy Mason Recreation Center) |
| 1907 | Charles Carroll Glover acquires much of present neighborhood from estate of Henry Kengla |
| c. 1910 | Hall Place row houses constructed, first major residential development; sold for approximately $5,000 |

## Glover Park Naming and Development

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1915 | Final plot sold at Holy Rood Cemetery |
| 1917 | The Observatory apartment building constructed at Wisconsin Avenue and Hall Place |
| 1921 | Charles Carroll Glover retires from Riggs Bank |
| 1922 | Wellington Apartment Hotel built at 2505 Wisconsin Avenue |
| 1924 | Charles Carroll Glover and Anne Mills Archbold donate 80+ acres creating Glover-Archbold Park |
| 1925 | William O. and Charles E. Cooley begin building houses in area |
| 1926 | Developer Benjamin H. Gruver first uses name "Glover Park" in advertisements |
| 1926 | Myron R. Walker moves to 2424 Tunlaw Road, first resident of the newly-named neighborhood |
| 1926 | First planned Breuninger home completed at 7707 13th Street |

## Institutional Development

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1932 | Stoddert Elementary School dedicated on November 7, confirming Glover Park as full-fledged neighborhood |
| 1932 | Glover Park Citizens Association founded |
| 1936 | Charles Carroll Glover dies (February 25); memorialized at National Cathedral |
| 1937 | Park and Shop Center opens at 2201 Wisconsin Avenue |

## War Era and Later Development

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1940s | Major development surge as federal workers move to DC for war effort; garden-style apartments built |
| 1942-present | Almost all new housing in Glover Park built as apartment buildings |
| 1950s | Larger apartment buildings constructed as DC population grows |
| 1954 | Industrial Home School residents transferred to Children's Center in Laurel, Maryland |
| 1954 | Georgetown Poor and Work House remnants (incorporated into Industrial Home School) razed |
| 1954 | 1902 schoolhouse preserved, becomes Guy Mason Recreation Center |

## Cold War and Modern Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1969 | US-Soviet agreement reached for embassy sites; Soviet Union leases Mount Alto property for 85 years |
| 1979 | Russian Embassy residential buildings, school, kindergarten, and sports fields completed |
| 1980 | The Meridian built at 2201 Wisconsin Avenue, replacing Park and Shop Center |
| 1980s | Georgetown University explores disinterring Holy Rood Cemetery bodies; blocked by legal action |
| 1984 | Sheffield Apartments built at 2320 Wisconsin Avenue |
| 1985 | Russian Embassy administrative and ceremonial buildings completed |
| 1980s | Last interment at Holy Rood Cemetery |

## Restoration and Recent History

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2005 | Glover Park Listserv migrates from Yahoo Groups to groups.io |
| 2018 | Georgetown University and Holy Trinity Church announce Holy Rood Cemetery restoration |
| November 2019 | Holy Trinity completes 645-niche columbarium at Holy Rood Cemetery |
| 2019 | Holy Rood Cemetery restoration completed with new gate, fence, landscaping, and reset headstones |

## Key Dates Summary

- **1808-1810**: First recorded settlement in Wilberforce village
- **1832**: Holy Rood Cemetery and Georgetown Poorhouse established
- **1890**: Streetcar service begins along Wisconsin Avenue
- **1907**: Charles Carroll Glover acquires neighborhood land
- **1926**: "Glover Park" name first used; residential development accelerates
- **1932**: Stoddert Elementary dedicated; GPCA founded
- **1954**: Guy Mason Recreation Center established
- **2019**: Holy Rood Cemetery restored
