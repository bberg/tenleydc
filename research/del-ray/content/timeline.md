# Del Ray, Alexandria: Historical Timeline

## Pre-Development Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 24, 1861 | Union military forces arrive in Alexandria; city becomes logistical supply center |
| 1861-1865 | Alexandria under military occupation throughout Civil War |
| 1862-1865 | Virginia Theological Seminary campus used as Union hospital; 500 soldiers buried on campus later relocated to Arlington National Cemetery |

## Streetcar and Development Era (1890s)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1890 | Washington, Alexandria & Mount Vernon Railway chartered, first running from Alexandria north to future Del Ray area |
| 1892 | Washington, Alexandria & Mount Vernon Railway established between Alexandria and Mount Vernon; one of first interurban electric railways in country |
| Early 1894 | Developer Charles Wood purchases 254 acres (Del Ray) for $38,900 and 39 acres (St. Elmo) for $15,314 |
| 1894 | Charles Wood plats Del Ray and St. Elmo on grid pattern independent of Old Town Alexandria |
| 1895 | Joseph Edward Supplee, 28-year-old printer from Maryland, moves to Howell Avenue |
| 1896 | First trains run from Alexandria to D.C. with stops at Braddock Road and Del Ray Avenue |
| 1896 | Railway line extended into Washington, crossing the Long Bridge (site of today's 14th Street Bridge) |

## Town of Potomac Era (1904-1930)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1904 | St. Asaph Racetrack closed following raid and prolonged community pressure led by Joseph Edward Supplee |
| 1904-1906 | Potomac Yard railroad classification yard constructed |
| 1906 | Potomac Yard opens: 450 acres, 52 miles of track, 1,400 employees; largest classification yard on East Coast |
| 1907 | Joseph Edward Supplee presides over meeting uniting Del Ray and St. Elmo residents |
| 1908 | Del Ray and St. Elmo incorporate as Town of Potomac with own mayor, council, police and fire service |
| 1908-1930 | Period of significance for Town of Potomac Historic District |
| 1915 | Photograph documents steam roller on Mount Vernon Avenue ripping up old tarmac surface at Windsor Avenue intersection |
| 1920 | Del Ray served by 33 daily trains; population growing as suburban living gains acceptance |
| 1920 | Parker-Gray School opens in Alexandria, teaching grades 1-8 for African American students |
| 1924 | Town of Potomac yearbook notes municipality limits real estate ownership to Caucasian persons |
| 1929 | Mount Vernon Avenue begins transition from rural/residential character to commercial main street |
| 1930 | City of Alexandria annexes Town of Potomac; town ceases to exist as independent municipality |
| 1930 | According to local accounts, residents burn town records rather than surrender them to Alexandria officials |

## Mid-20th Century

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1932 | Parker-Gray School expands to include three-year high school under Principal Wesley Elam; first high school for African Americans in Alexandria |
| 1935 | George Washington High School opens in Del Ray as new Art Deco building consolidating two white high schools |
| 1941 | End date for period of significance, Town of Potomac Historic District |
| 1950 | New Parker-Gray High School building opens |
| 1954 | Del Ray Citizens Association founded as non-profit 501(c)(4) |
| February 10, 1959 | Court-ordered desegregation begins; 14 Black children enter Alexandria's all-white schools |
| Post-desegregation | George Washington High School in Del Ray reaches 25 percent Black students |
| 1965 | Last class graduates from Parker-Gray High School |
| 1966 | Parker-Gray reopens as integrated middle school |

## Modern Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1989 | Potomac Yard railroad classification yard closes |
| 1991 | Town of Potomac Historic District listed on Virginia Landmarks Register |
| 1992 | Town of Potomac Historic District added to National Register of Historic Places |
| 1990s-Present | Art on the Avenue festival established, held first Saturday in October |
| Present | First Thursday series runs April-September |
| Present | Del Ray Farmers Market operates year-round on Saturdays |
| Present | Town of Potomac Historic District on Preservation Virginia's Most Endangered Historic Places list |

## Transportation Milestones

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1890 | Washington, Alexandria & Mount Vernon Railway chartered |
| 1892 | Electric railway service established between Alexandria and Mount Vernon |
| 1894 | Del Ray and St. Elmo platted as streetcar suburbs |
| 1896 | Railway extended into Washington via Long Bridge; stops at Braddock Road and Del Ray Avenue |
| 1906 | Potomac Yard opens, transforming Del Ray into railroad workers' community |
| 1908 | W&OD Railroad runs through Town of Potomac; Commonwealth Avenue station serves as streetcar transfer point |
| 1920 | 33 daily trains serve Del Ray |
| 1968 | W&OD Railroad ceases operations |
| 1989 | Potomac Yard classification yard closes after 83 years of operation |
| Present | Potomac Yard area undergoing redevelopment including Metro station |

## Civil War Context (Alexandria Region)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Spring 1861 | Virginia secedes from Union |
| May 24, 1861 | Federal troops arrive in Alexandria to take possession of city |
| 1861-1865 | Alexandria remains under military occupation |
| March 1862-August 1865 | Virginia Theological Seminary serves as Union base; buildings become hospitals housing 1,700 wounded troops |
| 1861-1865 | Fort Ward and ring of forts built for defense of Washington, D.C. |
| 1865 | 500 soldiers buried at Virginia Theological Seminary relocated to future Arlington National Cemetery |
