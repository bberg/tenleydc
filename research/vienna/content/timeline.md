# Vienna, Virginia: Historical Timeline

## Colonial and Early Settlement Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1754 | Colonel Charles Broadwater, colonial soldier and landowner, settles within Vienna town boundaries |
| 1767 | John Hunter (Broadwater's son-in-law) builds first recorded house, names it Ayr Hill after his birthplace in Scotland |
| 1852 | Benjamin Thornton acquires land in Vienna area from Reginald Fairfax; Thorntons own property until 1883 |
| 1854 | Dr. William Hendrick and family arrive from Phelps, New York (then called Vienna); community renamed Vienna |

## Railroad Era and Pre-Civil War

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 20, 1847 | Alexandria & Harper's Ferry Railroad Company incorporated |
| March 15, 1853 | Railroad renamed Alexandria, Loudoun & Hampshire Railroad |
| February 1855 | Railroad construction begins from Alexandria, passing through Falls Church and Vienna toward Leesburg |
| 1858 | Railroad reaches Vienna; town begins growth from farming community to village |
| 1859 | Freeman Store built by Abram and Susan Lydecker adjacent to railroad; houses general store, post office, fire department |

## Civil War Period (1861-1865)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 1861 | Virginia secedes from Union; Vienna votes 77-44 against secession (one of only three precincts to do so) |
| May 24, 1861 | Union troops seize the Alexandria, Loudoun & Hampshire Railroad |
| June 17, 1861 | Battle of Vienna: First railroad battle in warfare; Confederate forces under Colonel Maxcy Gregg ambush Union train carrying 1st Ohio Infantry under Brigadier General Robert C. Schenck; 8 killed, 4 wounded |
| 1861-1865 | Vienna changes hands multiple times between Union and Confederate forces |
| 1861-1865 | Freeman Store used by both armies as hospital and offices |

## Reconstruction and Town Development

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1866 | Major Orrin T. Hine, Union Army officer and Freedmen's Bureau agent, settles in Vienna |
| April-August 1867 | Major Hine serves as Assistant Superintendent at Vienna for Freedmen's Bureau |
| September 1, 1867 | First Baptist Church of Vienna founded; Major Hine presents deed to trustees George McBrown, Daniel West, and Edmund Harris for one dollar |
| 1867 | First church building erected at 315 Lawyers Road using Freedmen's Bureau lumber from demolished Union barracks |
| 1867 | Vienna Colored School opens in church building; first church in Vienna also serves as public school for Black students |
| 1870 | Virginia public school system established with segregation from beginning |
| 1885 | Major Orrin T. Hine owns substantial real estate; becomes leading advocate of public education |
| 1888 | Commuters fill trains; "every seat occupied, principally by government employees" traveling from Vienna and other points |
| 1890 | Vienna incorporated as town with population of 300; O.E. Hine elected first mayor |
| 1899 | Major Orrin T. Hine dies; served as mayor since incorporation |

## 20th Century

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1915 | Black teachers in Virginia paid one-third the salary of white teachers on average |
| 1923-1952 | Hunter's Branch serves as baptismal site for First Baptist Church of Vienna |
| 1939 | Louise Archer Elementary School opens as all-African-American elementary school |
| 1940 | Vienna population reaches only 1,237; town remains small and rural |
| Post-WWII | Suburban growth brings nearly 10,000 new residents |
| Late 1940s | Water and wastewater systems established |
| 1954 | Brown v. Board of Education declares school segregation unconstitutional |
| 1954-1959 | Virginia's "Massive Resistance" policy evades school desegregation |
| Late 1950s | Three African Americans attend predominantly white high schools in Fairfax County, including two at James Madison High School in Vienna |
| January 19, 1959 | Virginia State Supreme Court and U.S. Supreme Court declare Massive Resistance laws unconstitutional |
| 1961 | 100th anniversary Battle of Vienna reenactment featuring steam train on W&OD tracks |
| 1962 | Westbriar Civic Association founded; oldest continuously active civic association in Vienna |
| 1968 | Washington & Old Dominion Railroad ceases operations |
| 1969 | Town of Vienna purchases Freeman Store |

## Modern Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1974-1988 | W&OD Trail developed on former railroad right-of-way |
| January 1975 | Freeman Store restoration begins, returning building to Civil War-era appearance |
| 1975 | Northern Virginia Model Railroaders Club moves into Vienna Train Depot |
| 1976 | Historic Vienna, Inc. founded for preservation and education |
| 1977 | Northern Virginia Regional Park Authority begins purchasing W&OD right-of-way |
| 1982 | W&OD right-of-way purchase completed |
| 1987 | W&OD Trail designated National Recreation Trail by U.S. Department of the Interior |
| 1988 | W&OD Trail completed to Purcellville (45 miles) |
| 1995 | North East Vienna Citizens Association (NEVCA) founded |
| September 22, 2011 | Freeman Store listed on Virginia Landmarks Register |
| May 15, 2012 | Freeman Store listed on National Register of Historic Places |
| 2017 | First Baptist Church of Vienna celebrates 150th anniversary |
| 2020 | Census records Vienna population at 16,473 |
