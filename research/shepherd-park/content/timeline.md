# Shepherd Park Timeline

## Alexander Robey Shepherd Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| January 30, 1835 | Alexander Robey Shepherd born in Southwest Washington, D.C. |
| 1848 | Shepherd drops out of school at age 13, takes job as plumber's assistant |
| 1868 | Shepherd builds "Bleak House" mansion near corner of Floral and 14th Street, naming it after the Dickens novel |
| 1871 | Congress passes Organic Act creating unified DC government; Shepherd appointed to head Board of Public Works |
| 1873-1874 | Shepherd serves as Governor of the Territory of DC; transforms city with 157 miles of paved roads, 123 miles of sewers, 60,000 trees |
| 1874 | Congress audit reveals $13 million in debt; Congress declares bankruptcy on DC's behalf and takes back direct control |
| 1876 | Shepherd declares personal bankruptcy |
| 1880 | Shepherd relocates family to Mexico, purchases silver mine |
| September 12, 1902 | Alexander Robey Shepherd dies in Mexico |

## Neighborhood Development

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1917 | Sixteenth Street Heights Citizens Association founded (later renamed Shepherd Park Citizens Association) |
| 1926 | Developer Lewis E. Breuninger coins name "Shepherd Park" and proposes 200 single-family homes |
| 1926 | L.E. Breuninger and Sons, Inc. completes first planned home at 7707 13th Street in free Tudor style |
| 1926 | Gustav G. Loehler, John Gustav Loehler, and Lewis T. and Henry L. Breuninger develop five squares |
| 1926 | Restrictive covenants prohibit sale to Black and Jewish families |
| 1928 | Two one-room portable school buildings established near Kalmia Road |
| January 1932 | Alexander R. Shepherd Elementary School dedicated; descendants of Governor Shepherd participate |

## Jewish Community Growth

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1886 | Ohev Sholom Congregation founded as Chai Adon Congregation by Russian immigrants, oldest Orthodox congregation in DC |
| 1916 | Tifereth Israel Congregation founded by Russian, Polish, and Austrian immigrants at 2328 Ontario Road NW |
| 1940s | Jewish families begin moving to Shepherd Park in significant numbers after restrictive covenants become unenforceable |
| 1940s | Shepherd Park Citizens Association renamed to avoid confusion with Sixteenth Street Highlands |
| 1945 | Greater Washington Jewish population: 20,000 |
| 1948 | Supreme Court rules racially restrictive covenants unenforceable in Shelley v. Kraemer |
| 1940s-1950s | Jewish population reaches approximately 80% of neighborhood |
| Early 1950s | North Portal Estates developed by wealthy Jewish families following 1948 Supreme Court decision |
| 1953 | Jewish Community Council supports Thompson's restaurant desegregation case |
| 1956 | Greater Washington Jewish population reaches 81,000 (half in suburbs) |
| 1956 | Tifereth Israel Congregation (now 300 families) purchases land on upper 16th Street NW |
| 1957 | Tifereth Israel Congregation dedicates 550-seat synagogue in Shepherd Park |
| July 7, 1958 | Ohev Sholom and Talmud Torah merge, creating congregation of over 600 families |
| 1960 | Tifereth Israel affiliates with Conservative movement |
| November 27, 1960 | Ohev Sholom Congregation dedicates building on upper Sixteenth Street NW |
| 1970 | Greater Washington Jewish population reaches 110,000 |

## Integration and Civil Rights Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1957 | Marvin Caplan, Jewish journalist, moves to Manor Park |
| June 1958 | Neighbors, Inc. founded by interracial group after Manor Park citizens association refuses to admit Black members |
| 1958 | Marvin Caplan and Warren Van Hook (Black pharmacist) co-found Neighbors, Inc. with approximately 50 residents |
| 1958 | Neighbors, Inc. opens office above florist near "speculators row" on Kennedy Street |
| Late 1950s | African American families begin purchasing homes in Shepherd Park and surrounding areas |
| Late 1950s | Blockbusting campaign targets neighborhoods east of Rock Creek Park |
| 1958 | Shepherd Park Citizens Association and Neighbors, Inc. begin partnership to fight blockbusting |
| 1960-1963 | Neighbors, Inc. brings 60+ white families to Shepherd Park during period of citywide white population decline |
| 1960s | Neighbors, Inc. successfully ends Washington Post policy of labeling neighborhoods "Colored" |
| Early 1960s | Neighbors, Inc. blocks city freeway plan through community |
| 1960s-1970s | Shepherd Park Citizens Association and Neighbors, Inc. continue integration efforts |

## Marvin Caplan's National Impact

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1960s-1970s | Marvin Caplan serves as Executive Director of Leadership Conference on Civil Rights |
| 1964 | Caplan's strategic role in passing Civil Rights Act of 1964 |
| 1965 | Caplan's strategic role in passing Voting Rights Act of 1965 |
| 1968 | Caplan's strategic role in passing Fair Housing Act of 1968 |
| 1972 | Caplan's strategic role in Title IX's prohibition of gender discrimination |
| 2000 | Marvin Caplan dies |
| 2003 | Marvin Caplan Memorial Designation Act names park at Alaska Avenue NW and Holly Street NW |

## Modern Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1973 | Hanafi Muslim murders at 7700 16th Street NW; 7 family members of Hamaas Abdul Khaalis killed |
| 1990 | Juanita E. Thornton/Shepherd Park Library opens |
| 2005 | Walter Reed Army Medical Center closure announced |
| 2006 | PBS WETA Neighborhoods documentary on Neighbors, Inc. |
| 2013 | Ohev Sholom hires Maharat Ruth Friedman, first synagogue in US to hire a Maharat (Orthodox female spiritual leader) |

| April 14, 2019 | DC Public Library hosts Shepherd Park community documentation event |
| 2020s | Walter Reed redevelopment brings 2,100+ residential units, Harris Teeter, schools, retail |

## Key Dates Summary

- **1868**: Alexander Shepherd builds Bleak House
- **1917**: Sixteenth Street Heights Citizens Association founded
- **1926**: "Shepherd Park" name coined; restrictive covenant development begins
- **1948**: Supreme Court rules restrictive covenants unenforceable
- **1958**: Neighbors, Inc. founded to fight blockbusting
- **1960-1963**: Successful integration effort brings white families to neighborhood
- **1960**: Both Tifereth Israel and Ohev Sholom establish Shepherd Park synagogues
- **2003**: Marvin Caplan Park dedicated
