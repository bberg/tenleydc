# Woodley Park Timeline

A chronological guide to the key events that shaped Woodley Park, from colonial land grants through the development boom of the early twentieth century to the present day.

---

## Colonial and Early Republic Era (1700s-1800)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1700s | Land owned by **Colonel Ninian Beall**, Scottish immigrant; 795-acre tract stretches from Potomac River to future mansion site |
| Early 1790s | **Benjamin Stoddert** and **Uriah Forrest** purchase portions of land at request of George Washington to prevent speculation |
| 1793 | General **Uriah Forrest**, aide-de-camp to George Washington, builds **Rosedale** estate (now at 3501 Newark Street) |
| 1797 | **Philip Barton Key**, uncle of Francis Scott Key, purchases 250-acre wooded estate |

---

## The Estate Era (1801-1874)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1801 | **Woodley Mansion** built by Philip Barton Key in Federal style; named after Woodley Lodge in Berkshire, England |
| 1800s-1850s | Woodley Mansion serves as "summer White House" for Presidents **Martin Van Buren**, **Grover Cleveland**; reportedly also **John Tyler** and **James Buchanan** |
| Mid-1800s | **Gardiner Greene Hubbard**, future founder of National Geographic Society, maintains summer home in area |

---

## Early Development (1875-1905)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1875 | First subdivision called "Woodley" recorded; owned by Mrs. A.E. Kervand with winding Woodley Lane as main street |
| 1878 | Lots advertised as "2 to 10 acre lots, 20 minute walk from P Street Circle" |
| 1886 | **Department of Living Animals** established at Smithsonian with 185 animals; precursor to National Zoo |
| 1888 | Woodley Lane Bridge (iron truss) built to carry Connecticut Avenue over Rock Creek Park |
| 1889 | **National Zoological Park** established by Congress; signed into law by President Grover Cleveland |
| 1890 | Zoo becomes part of Smithsonian Institution |
| 1891 | **National Zoo** opens in Rock Creek Park; designed by landscape architect **Frederick Law Olmsted** |
| 1891 | First **Calvert Street Bridge** built (775-foot iron truss) by Rock Creek Railway; 125-foot-high iron trestles with wooden deck |
| July 20, 1891 | Calvert Street Bridge transferred to D.C. government |

---

## The Development Boom (1906-1945)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1906-1907 | **Taft Bridge** (Connecticut Avenue Bridge / "Million Dollar Bridge") completed; replaces 1888 Woodley Lane Bridge |
| 1905-1938 | **Period of Significance** for Woodley Park Historic District |
| 1911 | **Marthe Maret**, Swiss emigre, founds school teaching French to girls in Rhode Island Avenue apartment; sisters Jeanne and Louise join as teachers |
| 1913 | **St. Thomas Apostle Parish** founded; first pastor Fr. Thomas Walsh; masses offered in home on Cathedral Avenue |
| 1915 | Small white church built for St. Thomas parish on site of present rectory |
| 1918 | **Wardman Park Hotel** built by **Harry Wardman**; 1,200 rooms; dubbed "Wardman's Folly" |
| 1920s | Commercial development along Connecticut Avenue; foundation of St. Thomas church poured |
| 1920s-1930s | Mass offered in St. Thomas basement for almost 30 years; nicknamed "the church of the catacombs" |
| 1928 | **Wardman Tower** addition built; 8-story, 350-room annex designed by architect **Mihran Mesrobian** |
| 1929-1931 | **Kennedy-Warren Apartment Building** constructed; architect **Joseph Younger** (1894-1932) |
| 1931 | Wardman forced to sell hotel due to Great Depression |
| 1933-1935 | **Duke Ellington Bridge** (Calvert Street Bridge) rebuilt; designed by **Paul Philippe Cret** |
| 1935 | Kennedy-Warren northeast wing added |

---

## Mid-Century Era (1946-1980)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1947 | First televised broadcast of NBC's **Meet the Press** from Wardman Tower; host Lawrence Spivak lived there |
| 1951 | Cornerstone laid for St. Thomas Apostle Church |
| November 4, 1951 | St. Thomas Apostle Church dedicated by Archbishop Amleto Cicognani |
| 1952 | **Maret School** purchases Woodley Mansion (42 years after founding); sold by Phillips Academy, Andover, which inherited from Henry L. Stimson |
| 1953 | Sheraton Hotels purchases Wardman Park Hotel; renamed Sheraton-Park Hotel |
| 1964 | Motor Inn addition built at Sheraton-Park |
| 1974 | Calvert Street Bridge renamed **Duke Ellington Bridge** |
| 1979 | New $104 million **Sheraton Washington Hotel** opens; largest hotel and convention complex on East Coast |
| November 4, 1980 | President **Jimmy Carter** concedes election to Ronald Reagan in Sheraton ballroom |

---

## Metro Era and Preservation (1981-Present)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| December 5, 1981 | **Woodley Park-Zoo/Adams Morgan Metro Station** opens as part of 2.07-mile Red Line extension |
| 1981 | Metro station uses first four-coffer arch design (cost-saving measure); escalators have 102-foot vertical rise (longest in D.C.) |
| 1989 | Kennedy-Warren designated DC Historic Landmark |
| April 18, 1990 | Woodley Park Historic District added to DC Inventory of Historic Sites |
| June 15, 1990 | **Woodley Park Historic District** listed on National Register of Historic Places |
| 1994 | Kennedy-Warren listed on National Register of Historic Places |
| 2004 | Kennedy-Warren south wing completed (originally planned 1930s) |
| 2020 | Marriott Wardman Park closes |
| 2025 | Wardman Park demolished; site being redeveloped into 900 residential units |

## Key Figures

### Developers and Builders
- **Harry Wardman** - Wardman Park Hotel, numerous homes
- **Joseph Younger** (1894-1932) - Kennedy-Warren architect
- **Mihran Mesrobian** - Wardman Tower architect, Wardman's chief architect
- **Frederick Law Olmsted** - National Zoo landscape design

### Historical Figures
- **Philip Barton Key** - Built Woodley Mansion (1801)
- **General Uriah Forrest** - Built Rosedale (1793)
- **Henry L. Stimson** - Last private owner of Woodley; cabinet secretary
- **Gardiner Greene Hubbard** - National Geographic Society founder

### Presidential Connections
- **Martin Van Buren** - Summer residence at Woodley
- **Grover Cleveland** - Summer residence at Woodley
- **John Tyler** - Reportedly used Woodley as summer residence
- **James Buchanan** - Reportedly used Woodley as summer residence
