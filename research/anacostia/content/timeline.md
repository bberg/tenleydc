# Anacostia Historical Timeline

A chronological history of Anacostia, from Native American settlement through the present day.

## Pre-Colonial Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 4,000+ years ago | American Indians establish settlements along the Anacostia River, drawn to its abundant resources |
| Pre-1600s | The Nacochtank, an Algonquian-speaking tribe, give the river and area its name (anglicized as "Anacostia") |

## Founding Era (1850s)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| June 5, 1854 | **Uniontown founded** - Developers John Dobler, John Fox, and John W. Van Hook purchase 240 acres from Enoch Tucker for $19,000 through the Union Land Association |
| 1854 | Restrictive covenants prohibit sale of property to anyone of African or Irish descent |
| 1854 | John Van Hook builds his home on a nine-acre hilltop (later Cedar Hill); Dr. Charles H. Nichols, superintendent of the Government Hospital for the Insane, builds nearby |
| 1855-1859 | Cedar Hill mansion constructed by architect John Van Hook |
| By 1860 | Most Uniontown lots sold, but only four homes built per year on average |

## Post-Civil War Freedmen's Community (1860s-1870s)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1867 | **Barry Farm established** - Freedmen's Bureau purchases 375 acres from Julia Barry to house refugees from slavery |
| 1867 | Bureau subdivides farm into one-acre plots; freedmen can purchase over seven years |
| 1867-1869 | 266 families move to Barry Farm, many building homes by candlelight after working downtown |
| 1871 | Hillsdale School built by Barry Farm residents with Freedmen's Bureau sponsorship |
| 1871 | Lewis Douglass (Frederick's son) takes seat in D.C. House of Delegates; champions non-discrimination legislation |
| 1871 | Solomon Brown sponsors legislation changing Barry Farm's name to Hillsdale |
| 1873 | Union Land Association goes bankrupt in the Panic of 1873; Van Hook forced to sell Cedar Hill |
| 1873 | Residents succeed in getting act passed to rename neighborhood "Hillsdale" (name never officially adopted) |
| 1875 | Horse-drawn streetcar service reaches Anacostia, 13 years after the rest of the city |
| 1877 | **Frederick Douglass purchases Cedar Hill** for $6,700, defying racial covenants |
| 1879 | St. Teresa's Catholic Church built at 1244 V Street SE |

## Late 19th Century Development (1880s-1890s)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1880 | Census shows 15% of Uniontown residents are African American, despite original racial covenants |
| 1881 | Griswold's subdivision opens; Green's subdivision opens |
| 1886 | **Congress renames Uniontown to Anacostia** due to confusion with other "Uniontowns" nationwide |
| 1886 | Griswold's II subdivision opens |
| 1887 | Avalon subdivision opens |
| 1889 | **Birney Elementary School built** - First public school for African American children in the area, named for abolitionist James G. Birney |
| 1889 | Avalon Terrace subdivision opens |
| 1890 | Anacostia Addition subdivision opens |
| 1891 | Emanuel Episcopal Church (now Delaware Avenue Baptist Church) erected at 1301 V Street SE |
| 1892 | Bryan Place subdivision opens |
| 1894 | Griswold's III subdivision opens |
| 1895 | **Frederick Douglass dies at Cedar Hill** on February 20, after attending a women's rights rally with Susan B. Anthony |
| 1895 | Electric streetcars arrive in Anacostia |

## Early 20th Century (1900s-1940s)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1900 | Helen Pitts Douglass founds the Frederick Douglass Memorial and Historical Association |
| 1901 | Italian Renaissance building opens as James G. Birney Elementary School |
| 1908 | Ketcham School opens for white elementary students |
| 1916 | National Association of Colored Women's Clubs joins Cedar Hill preservation effort |
| 1920s | William Augustus Hazel, a Black architect, restores Cedar Hill - believed to be first historic preservation project by a Black architect in the United States |
| 1935 | Anacostia Junior-Senior High School opens for white students |
| 1941 | **Barry Farm Dwellings constructed** - Government seizes 34-acre section of community for segregated public housing for African Americans |

## Civil Rights Era (1949-1968)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Summer 1949 | **Youth help desegregate Anacostia swimming pool**, contributing to eventual desegregation of all DC recreation facilities |
| 1950 | Third Birney School opens at 2501 Martin Luther King Jr. Avenue |
| 1950 | Douglass Junior High opens for African American students in old Birney School building |
| Early 1950s | Barry Farm-Hillsdale parents at forefront of fight to desegregate schools |
| Early October 1954 | **White students at Anacostia, Eastern, and McKinley High Schools stage strikes** to protest school desegregation; police intervene |
| 1960s | Barry Farm Dwellings residents at forefront of welfare rights movement |
| 1962 | Cedar Hill established as a historic site |

## Modern Era (1970s-Present)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1978 | **Anacostia Historic District added to National Register of Historic Places** |
| 1988 | Cedar Hill designated as Frederick Douglass National Historic Site, administered by National Park Service |
| 2019 | **Barry Farm Dwellings demolished** to make way for new development |
| 2021 | Anacostia Historic District expanded; "Period of Significance" extended from 1854-1948 |

## Notable Residents

| Person | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| Frederick Douglass (1818-1895) | Internationally known writer, lecturer, newspaper editor, social reformer; U.S. Marshal for DC; U.S. Minister to Haiti; resided at Cedar Hill 1877-1895 |
| Anna Murray Douglass | Frederick's first wife; saved money as shoe mender that helped purchase Cedar Hill |
| Helen Pitts Douglass | Frederick's second wife; founded Frederick Douglass Memorial and Historical Association |
| Charles R. Douglass | Frederick's son; fought to open teaching jobs to African Americans and equalize wages |
| Lewis Douglass | Frederick's son; D.C. House of Delegates member; championed non-discrimination legislation |
| Solomon Brown | Smithsonian's sole Black professional employee for many years; elected to House of Delegates; helped rename Barry Farm to Hillsdale |
