# Falls Church, Virginia: Historical Timeline

## Colonial Era and Founding

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| c. 1699 | First English settlers establish at the site of modern Falls Church; "Big Chimneys" cottage built with date stone "1699" |
| 1729 | William Gunnell moves from Westmoreland County and acquires land |
| 1730 | Gunnell secures a cleric and convenes congregation meeting in his home |
| 1733 | First church building constructed |
| 1734 | The Falls Church formally founded at intersection of Native American trails (now Broad Street, Lee Highway, and Little Falls Street) |
| 1748 | George Mason named vestryman of The Falls Church |
| 1762 | George Washington named vestryman at age 30; original wood building fallen into decay |
| 1763 | Washington and George William Fairfax appointed wardens to oversee new church construction |
| 1767 | Construction of new brick church begins, designed by Colonel James Wren |
| 1769 | New brick church completed in late fall; enslaved laborers used in construction |
| Summer 1776 | Declaration of Independence read to citizens from the church steps |

## Revolution to Civil War

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1784 | Virginia revokes Anglican Church as state church |
| 1789 | The Falls Church abandoned; remains unoccupied for almost 50 years |
| 1836 | Church reoccupied by Episcopal congregation; Francis Scott Key serves as lay reader |
| 1838-1839 | Henry Fairfax uses personal funds to restore the church building |
| 1845 | Cherry Hill Farmhouse built by William Harvey in Greek Revival style |
| 1875 | Falls Church incorporated as a town; Joseph Riley leads petition effort |
| 1887 | Town votes to halve its size, annexing "colored settlement" south of Lee Highway to Fairfax County |
| 1888 | Washington, Arlington & Falls Church Railroad begins streetcar operations |

## Civil War Period (1861-1865)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 23, 1861 | Virginia votes to secede; Falls Church divided 44-26 in favor |
| June 1, 1861 | Battle of Fairfax Court House begins from Camp Union in Falls Church; first land engagement with fatal casualties |
| June 22, 1861 | Thaddeus Lowe conducts first aerial reconnaissance in U.S. military history from balloon Enterprise near Taylor's Tavern |
| September 1861 | Confederates silently withdraw from Falls Church to Centreville |
| September 29, 1861 | Union troops reoccupy Falls Church area |
| 1861 | Construction begins on Forts Taylor, Buffalo, Munson, and Ramsay as defensive outposts |
| 1862-1865 | The Falls Church used by Union army as hospital and stable |
| July 1861 | Thousands of Union soldiers march through Falls Church to First Manassas |
| October 1864 | Mosby's Rangers kill Frank Brooks (Black Home Guard member) and kidnap abolitionist John Read |

## Reconstruction and Jim Crow Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Early 1860s | Lay preacher John Read starts school for African Americans; daughter Betsy teaches reading and writing |
| Late 1800s | Charles and Mary Tinner purchase land on Tinner Hill; subdivide for their family |
| 1896 | Streetcar construction of Fairfax line begins |
| 1908 | Church significantly remodeled |
| 1908-1914 | Big Chimneys cottage demolished |
| January 8, 1915 | Edwin B. Henderson calls meeting of nine men at Joseph Tinner's home; Colored Citizens Protective League formed |
| 1918 | NAACP grants charter to "The Falls Church and Vicinity NAACP," first rural branch in U.S.; Joseph Tinner elected president |

## 20th Century

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1927 | Washington-Virginia Railway goes bankrupt; tracks sold at auction |
| 1928 | Joseph Tinner dies; Falls Church NAACP wins fight for public utilities for Black residents |
| January 17, 1932 | Last Arlington & Fairfax streetcar departs from 12th & D Streets, NW |
| 1936 | Evans Products Company purchases railway, replaces trolleys with auto-railers |
| September 9, 1939 | Last auto-railer runs; service replaced by buses |
| 1947 | Falls Church NAACP secures larger, more modern elementary school for Black students |
| 1948 | Falls Church achieves independent city status |
| 1949 | Falls Church NAACP wins door-to-door postal service for Black residents |
| 1955 | Falls Church NAACP secures public sanitary sewers and water for Black community |
| 1959 | Church galleries finally installed and new chancel added in most extensive renovation |
| 1968 | Falls Church NAACP wins street lights for Black neighborhood |

## Modern Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1973 | Cherry Hill Farmhouse placed on Virginia Landmarks Register and National Register of Historic Places |
| 1974 | First segment of W&OD Trail opens within Falls Church through agreement with Virginia Electric and Power Company |
| 1975 | Cherry Hill house and barn restored |
| 1976 | Cherry Hill dedicated as historic house museum for Bicentennial |
| 1991 | Falls Church News-Press founded by Nicholas F. Benton |
| 1997 | Tinner Hill Heritage Foundation founded |
| 1999 | Falls Church celebrates 300th anniversary; Tinner Hill Arch erected at Washington Avenue and Tinner Hill Road |
| 2002 | First annual Tinner Hill Blues Festival held |
| 2020 | Census records Falls Church population at 14,658 |
| 2023 | "Life and Times of the Falls Church News-Press" published by The History Press |

## Transportation Milestones

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1850s | W&OD Railroad first serves Falls Church area |
| 1888 | Washington, Arlington & Falls Church Railroad begins streetcar operations |
| 1896 | Fairfax streetcar line construction begins |
| 1927 | Washington-Virginia Railway goes bankrupt |
| 1932 | Arlington & Fairfax line loses right to travel into D.C. |
| 1939 | Auto-railer service ends; replaced by buses |
| 1968 | W&OD Railroad ceases operations |
| 1974 | W&OD Trail opens first segment in Falls Church |
| Present | East and West Falls Church Metro stations serve the area |
