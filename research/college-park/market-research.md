# College Park, Maryland - Market Research

## Executive Summary

College Park presents a unique opportunity in the local history content space. While the neighborhood has strong institutional resources (University of Maryland, Aviation Museum, National Archives), community-level historical content is fragmented across multiple civic associations and the exceptional Lakeland Community Heritage Project. The streetcar suburb angle and university town evolution offer rich untapped narratives.

---

## 1. Existing Websites and Blogs

### Official City Resources
- **City of College Park Official Website** ([collegeparkmd.gov](https://www.collegeparkmd.gov/)): Maintains a blog with community news, events, and city updates. Has a dedicated neighborhoods section covering all 17 distinct neighborhoods.
- **College Park City-University Partnership** ([collegeparkpartnership.org](https://collegeparkpartnership.org/)): Focuses on the Greater College Park initiative and community development, with some historical context about the city.

### Community News Publications
- **Streetcar Suburbs Publications** ([streetcarsuburbs.news](https://streetcarsuburbs.news/)): Award-winning nonprofit news organization covering the Route 1 corridor. Publishes the monthly "College Park Here & Now" newspaper. Excellent source of local history features and neighborhood spotlights.
- **College Park Patch** ([patch.com/maryland/collegepark](https://patch.com/maryland/collegepark)): Breaking news, local events, schools, weather, and community coverage.
- **Capital News Service** ([merrill.umd.edu/cns](https://merrill.umd.edu/cns)): Student-powered news from UMD's journalism school, occasionally covers local history.

### Specialized History Sites
- **Lakeland Community Heritage Project** ([lakelandchp.com](https://lakelandchp.com/)): Outstanding resource dedicated to preserving the history of African Americans in College Park. Features oral histories, photographic archives, and educational programs about this historic Black community established in 1890.
- **Lakeland Digital Archive** ([lakeland.umd.edu](https://lakeland.umd.edu/)): University of Maryland partnership providing digitized photographs, land records, census data, newspaper clippings, maps, and oral history recordings.

### Museums
- **College Park Aviation Museum** ([fieldoffirsts.com](https://www.fieldoffirsts.com/)): Smithsonian affiliate documenting the world's oldest continuously operating airport (since 1909). Extensive library and archives on early aviation history.

## 2. Historical Societies

### Local Organizations
- **Lakeland Community Heritage Project, Inc.**: Formed in 2007 to preserve Lakeland's history. Published "Images of America: Lakeland: African Americans in College Park." Active in educational outreach and community programming.
- **Prince George's County Historical Society**: Regional organization covering College Park among other communities. Partners with local preservation efforts.

### State and Regional Resources
- **Maryland Center for History and Culture** (formerly Maryland Historical Society): State's oldest cultural institution (founded 1844). Houses extensive Maryland collections including 60,000 books, 800,000 photographs, and 5 million manuscripts.
- **Montgomery History** (for nearby Montgomery County comparisons)

### University Resources
- **University of Maryland Department of American Studies**: Maintains the Lakeland Digital Archive partnership since 2009. Has received NEH grants for preservation work.

## 3. Civic Associations

College Park has **17 distinct neighborhoods**, each with civic associations:

### Most Active with History Content
- **Old Town College Park Civic Association (OTCPCA)**: Led by Kathy Bryant. Historic neighborhood between Calvert Road and Rossborough Lane. Maintains a listserv for community connection. Located in locally designated historic district.
- **Calvert Hills Citizens Association** ([calverthills.weebly.com](https://calverthills.weebly.com/)): National Registry historic district. Hosts traditional events including Halloween Parade, Ice Cream Social, and Holiday Tree Lighting. $20 annual dues.
- **Lakeland Civic Association** ([lakelandchp.com/lakeland-civic-association](https://lakelandchp.com/lakeland-civic-association/)): Works on community design, programming, and redevelopment. Strong ties to heritage preservation.
- **North College Park Community Association (NCPCA)** ([myncpca.org](https://myncpca.org/)): Promotes civic welfare for North College Park. Strictly non-partisan and non-sectarian.
- **College Park Estates Civic Association (CPECA)**: Less formal organization between Lake Artemesia and Kenilworth Avenue. Works with neighboring Yarrow on events.

### Historic Districts
- **Old Town College Park Historic District**: Locally designated historic district
- **Calvert Hills**: National Registry district with Colonial Revival, Queen Anne, Craftsman, and Tudor Revival homes

## 6. Real Estate Coverage

### History in Marketing
Real estate listings frequently mention:
- **Streetcar suburb heritage**: Connection to DC via historic trolley lines
- **Architectural diversity**: Colonials, bungalows, Foursquares, ramblers, Queen Annes
- **Neighborhood development timelines**: Lakeland (1890s), Berwyn (1891), Daniels Park (1905), Hollywood (1920s-50s)
- **University connection**: UMD presence since 1930s

### Key Marketing Points
- Tree-lined streets with mature trees
- Red-brick colonials with sash windows and shutters
- Double sidewalks in historic areas
- Proximity to two Metro stations
- Tech corridor growth

### Pricing Context
Prince George's County was heavily impacted by the mid-2000s real estate crash, making College Park prices competitive compared to other DC suburbs.

### Real Estate Sites with History Content
- **The Foley Group/Compass** ([thefoleygroupofcompass.com](https://thefoleygroupofcompass.com/)): Neighborhood guides with historical context
- **Northrop Realty**: Prince George's County listings with area information

## 8. Content Gaps Identified

### Major Gaps
1. **Individual Neighborhood Histories**: Beyond Lakeland, most neighborhoods lack dedicated historical documentation
2. **Streetcar Era**: Limited content on how trolley lines shaped development
3. **African American Communities Beyond Lakeland**: Other Black communities along Route 1 underrepresented
4. **Commercial History**: Route 1 business district evolution poorly documented
5. **Post-WWII Development**: 1950s-1970s suburban growth not well covered
6. **Urban Renewal Impacts**: Beyond Lakeland, urban renewal effects on other communities
7. **Women's History**: Limited documentation of women's contributions to College Park
8. **Immigration and Diversity**: Modern demographic shifts and community stories
9. **B&O Railroad Impact**: Railroad's role in community development underexplored
10. **Sports and Recreation History**: Washington Senators 1943 spring training, local athletics

### Underserved Audiences
- Residents new to College Park seeking neighborhood history
- Students wanting to understand the community beyond campus
- Preservationists needing documentation for older homes
- Genealogists researching College Park families

## 9. Unique Angles for Coverage

### Underserved Stories

1. **The Trolley Trail**: How the streetcar lines physically connected and shaped College Park neighborhoods; the transition from streetcar suburb to auto suburb

2. **Aviation Firsts**: Beyond the museum - personal stories of aviators, the community's relationship with the airport, early aviation experiments

3. **Urban Renewal's Long Shadow**: Comprehensive look at how urban renewal affected not just Lakeland but the entire Route 1 corridor

4. **The University Town Evolution**: How College Park transformed from farming community to streetcar suburb to college town to emerging tech hub

5. **Lake Artemesia and Lost Waters**: The lakes that gave Lakeland its name; the goldfish farm; recreation history

6. **Route 1 Architecture**: A visual tour of surviving historic commercial and residential architecture

7. **The B&O Connection**: How the railroad (1835) predated and enabled all subsequent development

8. **Lesser-Known Historic Figures**: Beyond Wilbur Wright, who else shaped College Park?

9. **The 17 Neighborhoods**: Individual histories of each of College Park's distinct communities

10. **Integration and Civil Rights**: College Park's role in civil rights history, school integration, community relations

## 10. Positioning Opportunity

### Competitive Analysis
- **Lakeland Community Heritage Project**: Excellent but narrowly focused on one community
- **Aviation Museum**: Strong institutional history, less community focus
- **Streetcar Suburbs News**: Current events focused, occasional history features
- **City Website**: Administrative focus, limited historical depth

### Differentiation Strategy

**Recommended Position**: "The Complete History of College Park's 17 Neighborhoods"

**Key Differentiators**:
1. **Comprehensive scope**: Cover ALL neighborhoods, not just the famous ones
2. **Community voices**: Oral histories and resident stories beyond academic/institutional sources
3. **Visual storytelling**: Historic photos, maps, and then-and-now comparisons
4. **Streetcar suburb narrative**: Position College Park within the larger DC streetcar suburb story
5. **Accessible format**: Make academic research accessible to general readers
6. **Connection to present**: Link historic themes to current community issues

**Content Opportunities**:
- Partner with civic associations for neighborhood-specific content
- Create walking tour guides for each historic area
- Document endangered historic sites before redevelopment
- Interview longtime residents for oral history project
- Map the evolution of Route 1 commercial district

**Target Audience**:
- New residents seeking community connection
- UMD students and alumni
- History enthusiasts and preservationists
- Real estate professionals
- Local educators and students
