# Hyattsville Timeline

A chronological history of Hyattsville, Maryland, from earliest human habitation to the present day.

---

## Pre-Colonial and Colonial Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| c. 8,000 years ago | Archaeological record indicates human habitation begins in the area |
| Pre-contact | Nacotchtank/Anaquashtank people (Piscataway-speaking Algonquian group) live along the upper Anacostia River |
| 1608 | First European exploration of the area |
| 1680s | Nacotchtank largely moved away and merged with other tribes due to European encroachment and disease |
| 1720s | John Beall acquires land and establishes Beall Town |

---

## Early Development and Founding (1807-1885)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1807 | Christopher Hyatt sells large tract of land to his 8-year-old son, Christopher Clark Hyatt |
| 1812 | Maryland authorizes construction of Washington and Baltimore Turnpike (modern US Route 1) |
| 1828 | Baltimore and Ohio Railroad chartered |
| 1835 | B&O Railroad Washington Branch connects Washington and Baltimore; fundamental to Hyattsville's development |
| 1844 | Samuel Morse sends first telegraph test message from area near Hyattsville |
| March 19, 1845 | Christopher Clark Hyatt purchases first parcel of land (3 acres, part of Black Ask tract); builds 32-room mansion, opens store |
| 1859 | Hyatt names community "Hyattsville" in his postmaster application |
| 1874 | Alexandria Branch (Extension) of B&O Railroad built, starting from Hyattsville and ending at Shepherd's Landing ferry |
| 1880 | Ten daily trains stop at Hyattsville station |
| 1884 | Ornate B&O Railroad station built; designed by E. Francis Baldwin, B&O's official architect; largest and most ornate station between Baltimore and Washington |

---

## Incorporation and Streetcar Era (1886-1920)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| April 7, 1886 | City of Hyattsville incorporated |
| 1887 | Hyattsville Building Association founded by Charles H. Welsh and others; finances early development |
| 1887 | First African American Civil War veterans purchase lots in what becomes North Brentwood from Captain Wallace A. Bartlett |
| 1887 | McEwen House built from mail-order design |
| 1889-90 | Welsh House built for banker Charles H. Welsh |
| 1890 | Hyattsville population exceeds 1,000; one of only two cities in Prince George's County at this size |
| 1891 | Henry Randall purchases first lots in northern section of North Brentwood |
| 1893 | Streetcar first connects Hyattsville to Washington, D.C. |
| 1897 | Maryland and Washington Railway begins streetcar service from Florida and New York Avenues, NE to Mt. Rainier |
| 1898 | Mergers create City & Suburban Railway |
| 1899 | Streetcar service extended to Riverdale |
| 1900 | Streetcar service extended to Berwyn |
| 1902 | First through streetcar service from Washington to Laurel via Mt. Rainier, Brentwood, North Brentwood, Hyattsville, Riverdale Park, College Park, Berwyn, Branchville, and Beltsville |
| 1918 | Hyattsville Armory built |

---

## Interwar and World War II Era (1921-1945)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 7, 1924 | North Brentwood incorporated as first African American-majority municipality in Maryland; Jeremiah Hawkins elected first mayor (first Black mayor in Maryland) |
| 1927 | William Pinkney Magruder donates Magruder Park to city with deed restricting use to "Caucasian inhabitants only" |
| 1928-1950 | Black students from Hyattsville attend Lakeland High School in College Park |
| 1930s | U.S. Post Office built on Gallatin Street |
| 1944 | Additional restrictive covenants added when expanding Magruder Park |

---

## Post-War Era (1946-1979)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1948 | Supreme Court rules in Shelley v. Kraemer that racial covenants are unenforceable |
| 1950 | Black students begin attending Fairmount Heights High School |
| 1950s | B&O Railroad station demolished |
| 1957 | Metal barrier erected on Windom Road to segregate white Brentwood from Black North Brentwood |
| 1958 | Streetcar service to Hyattsville ends |
| 1964 | Prince George's County schools integrate |
| 1977 | Violence follows July Fourth celebration; fireworks cancelled |

---

## Modern Era (1980-Present)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| February 14, 1980 | Hyattsville Preservation Association (HPA) founded |
| 1982 | Hyattsville Historic District listed on National Register of Historic Places; HPA incorporated |
| 1986 | Fireworks return to Magruder Park for city's 100th anniversary celebration |
| 1996 | Gateway Arts District initiative launched |
| 1997 | Anacostia Trails Heritage Area, Inc. founded |
| 2003 | North Brentwood Historic District listed on National Register of Historic Places |
| 2004 | Hyattsville Historic District expanded to include over 1,000 structures |
| 2012 | Hyattsville Wire launched; Anacostia Trails Heritage Area launches "Maryland Milestones" tourism brand |
| July 2016 | Jim Henson memorial sculpture "Muppet Dawn" installed in Magruder Park |
| 2017 | Brentwood and North Brentwood begin reimagining Windom Road barrier site as Historic Barrier Park |
| April 2020 | David C. Driskell dies of COVID-19 |
| May 4, 2021 | Hyattsville City Council votes 9-1 to rename Magruder Park as David C. Driskell Community Park |
| 2024 | North Brentwood celebrates centennial of incorporation |

---

## Transportation Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1812 | Washington-Baltimore Turnpike authorized |
| 1835 | B&O Railroad Washington Branch opens |
| 1874 | Alexandria Branch built from Hyattsville |
| 1884 | Ornate Hyattsville station built |
| 1893 | Streetcar service begins to Washington |
| 1897 | Maryland and Washington Railway begins service |
| 1902 | Through service to Laurel established |
| 1950s | Railroad station demolished |
| 1958 | Streetcar service ends |
| 1962 | Region-wide streetcar system completely dismantled |
| Present | West Hyattsville Metro station serves community; Rhode Island Avenue Trolley Trail preserves streetcar right-of-way |

## Architectural Timeline

| Date | Period/Style |
|------|--------------|
| Late 1880s | Victorian houses built; Second Empire, Queen Anne, Italianate styles |
| 1887 | McEwen House (mail-order design) |
| 1889-90 | Welsh House (Queen Anne elements) |
| Late 1910s-early 1940s | Sears bungalows and Arts & Crafts houses |
| 1918 | Armory built |
| 1930s | Post Office on Gallatin Street |
| 1950s-60s | Cape Cod cottages, ramblers, split-levels |
| 1982 | Historic District established |
| 2004 | Historic District expanded |
