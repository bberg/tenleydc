# Neighborhood Selection Analysis

> Systematic evaluation of 100+ DMV neighborhoods to identify the best 15 targets for the site network.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

### Primary Factors (Weighted)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Search Volume** | 25% | Monthly searches for "[Neighborhood] history", "[Neighborhood] DC", etc. |
| **Historical Richness** | 20% | Depth of available historical content, notable events, landmarks |
| **Competition Gap** | 20% | Lack of existing quality neighborhood sites |
| **Content Availability** | 15% | Newspaper archives, LOC images, landmark nominations |
| **Population/Traffic** | 10% | Neighborhood size and visitor interest |
| **Domain Availability** | 10% | Good .com domains available |

### Secondary Factors

- Proximity to existing AU Park site (network effects)
- Community engagement level (historical societies, civic groups)
- Distinctive character/story
- Cross-neighborhood connections (shared history with other targets)

## Selection Criteria Details

### What makes a GOOD target:

- **High search interest** but **low competition** (underserved demand)
- **Rich historical content** available in archives
- **Distinctive story** that differentiates from other neighborhoods
- **Active community** that would engage with the site
- **Good domain available** (.com or clear alternative)
- **Network synergy** with other selected neighborhoods

### What makes a POOR target:

- Already has excellent neighborhood coverage (e.g., Georgetown has extensive resources)
- Limited historical distinctiveness (newer development, less story)
- Low search volume (no demand)
- Domain unavailable and no good alternatives
- Overlaps significantly with adjacent neighborhood (redundant)
