History, culture, and community in Hyattsville, Maryland

Hyattsville, Maryland is a city in Prince George's County with a rich history dating back to the 1840s. Known today for its vibrant Arts District, historic neighborhoods, and diverse community, Hyattsville has transformed from a railroad suburb to a thriving urban center.

History

Hyattsville was founded by Christopher Clark Hyatt, a merchant who established a store near the Baltimore and Ohio Railroad in 1845. The arrival of the railroad transformed the area from farmland into a thriving suburb, attracting residents from Washington who sought fresh air and open spaces.

The city was incorporated in 1886 and quickly developed a commercial center along Baltimore Avenue. Victorian-era homes and commercial buildings from this period still stand in the historic district, representing some of the finest architecture in Prince George's County.

Arts District

Today, Hyattsville is home to a thriving Arts District that has transformed the Route 1 corridor. Galleries, studios, restaurants, and creative businesses have revitalized historic buildings, making the area a destination for arts and culture in the region.

Modern Hyattsville

The city continues to evolve with new transit-oriented development, including the Prince George's Plaza Metro station area. Hyattsville balances preservation of its historic character with thoughtful development to serve its diverse, growing community.
