# College Park Timeline

A chronological history of College Park, Maryland, from earliest human habitation to the present day.

---

## Pre-Colonial Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 3000-2600 B.C. | Earliest evidence of human activity; archaeological excavations south of Archives II recover Clagett and Vernon style projectile points |
| Pre-1600s | Piscataway peoples inhabit the region; early European settlers later find relics of Indian settlement while digging irrigation ditches along the Eastern Branch (Anacostia River) |

---

## Colonial and Early American Period (1600s-1855)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 17th-18th centuries | European settlers establish large plantations on land grants from Lord Baltimore |
| 1794 | Baron Henri Joseph Stier, a Belgian aristocrat fleeing the French Revolution, arrives in Maryland |
| Early 1800s | Stier builds Riversdale mansion on 739-acre plantation |
| 1803 | Rossborough Inn completed; oldest standing building in present-day College Park |
| c. 1820 | Baltimore-Washington Turnpike (modern Route 1) constructed, enabling stagecoach travel |
| 1835 | Baltimore & Ohio Railroad Washington Branch arrives; College Station established as stop |

---

## Founding and Early Development (1856-1899)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 6, 1856 | Maryland Agricultural College chartered by Maryland General Assembly |
| 1858 | Charles Benedict Calvert purchases 420 acres for college campus for $21,000 |
| October 5, 1859 | First 34 students enter Maryland Agricultural College; Joseph Henry, Secretary of the Smithsonian Institution, delivers keynote address |
| February 1864 | Maryland Agricultural College becomes land grant institution under Morrill Act |
| April 1864 | General Ambrose E. Burnside and 6,000 Union soldiers of the Ninth Army Corps camp on campus |
| 1866 | Maryland legislature assumes half ownership of the college after bankruptcy |
| 1872 | Original College Park subdivision platted by Eugene Campbell; local post office called "College Lawn" |
| 1880 | Ten daily trains in each direction stop in Hyattsville; eight stop in College Park |
| 1887 | Agricultural Experiment Station established at the college |
| 1889 | College Park re-platted by John O. Johnson and Samuel W. Curriden on 125 acres purchased from Ella Campbell, a Calvert heir |
| 1890 | Lakeland neighborhood developed by Edwin A. Newman as resort-style suburban community |
| 1891 | Berwyn neighborhood begins development |

---

## Streetcar Suburb Era (1900-1944)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1899 | "Maryland Line" streetcar opens service from downtown Washington to Hyattsville |
| 1900 | Rhode Island Avenue streetcar extended into College Park, terminating in Branchville; streetcar reaches Berwyn |
| 1902 | Streetcar service reaches Laurel |
| 1903 | Lakeland established as African American community with public school, First Baptist Church, and Embry AME Church |
| 1905 | Daniels Park neighborhood platted |
| August 1909 | College Park Airport established by United States Army Signal Corps |
| October 8, 1909 | Wilbur Wright begins training first military pilots at College Park |
| October 26, 1909 | Lt. Frank Lahm and Lt. Frederic Humphreys fly solo for first time, becoming first U.S. military pilots |
| October 27, 1909 | Mrs. Ralph Henry Van Deman becomes first American woman to fly in powered aircraft |
| November 2, 1909 | Wilbur Wright makes his last public flight at College Park, setting speed record of 46 mph |
| November 3, 1909 | Lt. George C. Sweet becomes first Naval officer to fly |
| 1911 | Nation's first military aviation school opens at College Park Airport |
| 1916 | Maryland State College (formerly Maryland Agricultural College) taken over fully by state; first female students enroll |
| 1918 | Post Office begins airmail service between Washington, Philadelphia, and New York from College Park Airport |
| April 9, 1920 | Maryland State College becomes University of Maryland |
| 1920 | Emile and Henry Berliner make first vertical take-off (controlled helicopter flight) at College Park |
| 1920s-1950s | Hollywood neighborhood developed |
| 1928 | Lakeland High School opens; serves as only high school for African Americans in northern Prince George's County |

---

## Post-War Era (1945-1992)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1945 | College Park incorporated as a town |
| 1948 | Streetcar service shortened to run only to Beltsville |
| 1950 | Lakeland High School closes; students transferred to Fairmount Heights High School |
| 1955 | College Park becomes a city |
| 1956 | Streetcar service runs only to Berwyn |
| September 7, 1958 | Streetcar service to College Park ends |
| 1959 | Municipal center built |
| 1960s-1970s | Urban renewal program uses eminent domain to take two-thirds of Lakeland's territory; approximately 65 households relocated |

---

## Modern Era (1993-Present)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1993 | Metro Green Line service begins to College Park |
| 2007 | Lakeland Community Heritage Project, Inc. formed to preserve community history |
| 2009 | University of Maryland partners with LCHP to create Lakeland Digital Archive |
| 2012 | Old Town College Park Historic District listed on National Register of Historic Places (213 contributing properties) |
| 2023 | Maryland Historical Trust erects historical marker for Lakeland community |
