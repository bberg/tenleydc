Kalorama Almanac

The Beautiful View: Washington's most prestigious address

Kalorama takes its name from the Greek word meaning "beautiful view," chosen by poet and diplomat Joel Barlow in 1807. That view, and the elegance it suggested, has defined the neighborhood ever since. Today Kalorama is one of Washington's most prestigious addresses, home to embassies, former presidents, and some of the finest examples of early twentieth-century residential architecture in the United States.

History

Kalorama's transformation from rural estate to urban neighborhood took place over more than a century. The original Kalorama mansion, built around 1750 and renamed by Joel Barlow, anchored a property that passed through the hands of some of early America's most prominent figures.

The Great Depression brought an unexpected transformation. Wealthy families sold their mansions to foreign governments seeking diplomatic residences. France was the first nation to purchase property for an embassy. Today Kalorama contains dozens of embassies, earning its place on Embassy Row.

Presidential Connections

Six U.S. presidents have lived in Sheridan-Kalorama: William Howard Taft, Woodrow Wilson, Warren G. Harding, Herbert Hoover, Franklin D. Roosevelt (as a young man), and Barack Obama. Woodrow Wilson's S Street house is now Washington's only presidential residence open to the public.
