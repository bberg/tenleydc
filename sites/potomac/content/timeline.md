# Potomac Timeline

A chronological history of Potomac, Maryland, from earliest human habitation to the present day.

---

## Pre-Colonial Era

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| c. 14,000 years ago | First humans inhabit the Potomac River region |
| 1200-1500 A.D. | Algonquin Indian site occupied; first such site documented by Maryland state historians |
| Pre-contact | Patawomeck village exists at Indian Point; village name gives river its name |

---

## Colonial Period (1714-1799)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1714 | Edward Offutt receives 600-acre land grant of "Clewerwell" from Lord Baltimore; first European settlement |
| 1716 | Tehogee Indian Trail established by Canaze nation as trade route near Offutt's land grant |
| 1785 | George Washington founds the Patowmack Canal Company to make the Potomac River navigable |
| 1785-1802 | Patowmack Company uses mostly enslaved labor to construct five skirting canals at Great Falls |

---

## Antebellum and Civil War Era (1800-1874)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1820 | First tavern established in the Offutts Crossroads community |
| 1826-1831 | Great Falls Tavern constructed by C&O Canal Company |
| 1828 | Chesapeake and Ohio Canal Company formed, taking over Patowmack Company assets |
| 1829 | Lockkeeper's house built at Lock 20 (later incorporated into Great Falls Tavern) |
| 1830 | Crommelin House (hotel) established at Lock 20; guests could stay for 25 cents per night |
| 1831 | C&O Canal begins operations |
| c. 1860 | Offutts Crossroads contains two general stores, blacksmith shop, and post office serving approximately 100 residents |

---

## Post-Civil War and Rural Community Era (1875-1919)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 1875 | William Davis purchases first 4 acres of Tobytown for $80 from John and Susan Rowzee |
| 1875 | Tobytown founded by emancipated African Americans William Davis, Elsie Martin, and Emory Genies; one of approximately 40 such communities in Montgomery County |
| 1881 | John McDonald petitions to rename Offutts Crossroads to "Potomac" |
| 1884 | Martin family dedicates land for Tobytown Meeting House (church and school) |
| 1887 | Tobytown church begins doubling as schoolhouse |
| 1888 | Pleasant View Methodist Episcopal Church established |
| 1889 | Great flood damages C&O Canal structures; floodwaters reach second floor of Great Falls hotel |
| 1890s | Tobytown has its own school and church; William Davis expands holdings to 93 acres |
| 1898 | Gibson Grove AME Zion Church established in nearby Cabin John |
| 1902 | Thomas Perry builds house at Falls and River Roads; establishes general store |

---

## Equestrian Transformation Era (1920-1949)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1920s | Financier Lyman Kendall builds Kentsdale mansion in Italian Renaissance style; first appearance of ostentatious wealth |
| 1921 | Congressional Country Club founded by Indiana Republican congressmen Oscar E. Bland and O.R. Luhring |
| 1924 | C&O Canal closes after nearly a century of operation |
| 1924 | Congressional Country Club opens |
| 1930s | Depression-era farmers sell land to Washington, D.C. equestrians; Potomac transforms from farm community to horse country |
| 1938 | National Park Service takes over C&O Canal property |
| 1940s | Main horse barn at future Avenel site constructed; reportedly largest single-story barn in Maryland |
| 1948 | Fire destroys portions of Great Falls Tavern; NPS restores building and opens museum |

---

## Suburban Development Era (1950-1999)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1950s | Suburbanization begins due to proximity to Washington, D.C.; rapid transformation from rural to exclusive suburb |
| 1956-1973 | Potomac Overlook developed by merchant builder Edmund J. Bennett and architects Keyes, Lethbridge & Condon |
| 1961 | Potomac Overlook receives awards from American Institute of Architects and National Association of Home Builders |
| 1964 | Potomac Woods Citizens Association founded |
| 1965 | Journalist exposes Tobytown's substandard conditions: tar paper shanties, non-potable water, single outhouse |
| 1965-1972 | Community mobilization, VISTA volunteers, businesses, and government provide support to Tobytown |
| 1971 | Fox Hills West Citizens Association incorporated |
| 1972 | Montgomery County Public Housing Authority purchases Tobytown land |
| 1973 | 26 federally subsidized clapboard homes replace razed shanties in Tobytown |
| 1980s | Avenel planned community developed on thousand-acre former farm |
| 1986 | TPC Potomac at Avenel Farm opens (May); Chrysler Cup held, captained by Arnold Palmer |
| 1987 | Kemper Open begins at TPC Potomac |
| 1988 | North Potomac Citizens Association formed |
| 1996 | Avenel receives Urban Land Institute "Award for Excellence" for best planned community in nation |

---

## Modern Era (2000-Present)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2008 | CNNMoney ranks Potomac #7 top-earning town in America |
| 2016 | Bus service finally extended to Tobytown, ending decades of isolation |
| April 19, 2022 | Montgomery County Council designates Potomac Overlook as Historic District |
| 2026 | Montgomery County prepares for 250th anniversary (semiquincentennial) |

---

## Related Timeline: Glen Echo and Cabin John

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1853-1863 | Cabin John Bridge constructed, enclosing nine-foot-diameter brick-lined water conduit |
| 1885 | Moses Hall built in Cabin John; center of African American community life |
| 1889 | Brothers Edwin and Edward Baltzley purchase over 500 acres, found Glen Echo |
| 1891 | National Chautauqua of Glen Echo established |
| 1892 | Glen Echo Chautauqua disbands after rumored malaria epidemic |
| 1897 | Clara Barton moves to Glen Echo; lives there until death in 1912 |
| 1911 | Glen Echo Amusement Park opens |
| 1940s | Cabin John Gardens built for David Taylor Model Basin workers |
| 1956 | Cabin John Gardens converted to cooperative housing |
| Late 1960s | Glen Echo Amusement Park closes |
| Present | Glen Echo Park operates as arts center under National Park Service |
