# H Street Historical Timeline

A chronological history of the H Street NE corridor, from its founding through destruction and revival.

## Founding and Early Development (1849-1912)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1849 | **H Street NE corridor built** - One of Washington's earliest commercial districts |
| 1850s | Swampoodle settlement develops around H Street and North Capitol, providing workforce for major construction projects |
| 1870 | Columbia Railway Company establishes streetcar route along H Street |
| 1872 | Streetcars arrive, spurring commercial development and bringing customers from across the city |
| 1888 | Trinidad neighborhood established, named after Caribbean island where landowner James Barry had lived |
| 1897 | **Northeast Temple and Market built** (1119-1123 H Street NE) - First buildings electrified on H Street NE |
| 1907 | Union Station construction begins, splitting H Street at Delaware Avenue with railway tracks |

## Commercial Peak Era (1913-1949)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| September 10, 1913 | **Apollo Theater opens** (624 H Street NE) with silent movies; designed by C. Clark Jones in Renaissance style; part of Crandall network |
| 1929 | First Sears Roebuck store in Washington opens on Bladensburg Road (several blocks east of H Street business center) |
| 1931 | **Carroll Barber Shop opens** next to Greenbaum's - One of H Street's first African American businesses |
| August 31, 1938 | **Atlas Theater opens** (1333 H Street NE) with Mickey Rooney in "Love Finds Andy Hardy"; designed by John Jacob Zink in Art Deco style; built by Kogod-Burka chain |
| 1938 | Atlas Theater is one of four movie houses on H Street; first new theater in central Northeast in 25 years |
| 1943 | **Plymouth Theater opens** (1365 H Street) in old auto showroom - Serves African American patrons excluded from white theaters |
| 1949 | **Buses replace streetcars** after 100 years of service on H Street |

## Segregation and Civil Rights (1950s)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1951 | Jake Napier opens **Ultra-Modern Barbershop** (1338 H Street) - Will operate for nearly 50 years |
| 1953 | Supreme Court forces integration of public accommodations; Atlas Theater desegregates after being "whites only" |
| 1955 | Apollo Theater demolished |

## The Fire and Its Aftermath (1968-2001)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| April 4, 1968 | Martin Luther King Jr. assassinated in Memphis |
| April 4-8, 1968 | **H Street NE looted and burned** from Benning Road to Hopscotch Bridge; witnesses see flames from Florida Avenue |
| 1968 | City-wide: 13 killed, ~1,000 injured, 6,100+ arrested, 1,200 buildings burned; 13,000+ soldiers patrol the capital |
| 1968 | Two music stores on H Street relocate to Arlington and Wheaton; never rebuild on H Street |
| 1970s-1980s | **Decades of disinvestment** expose area to crime and decay; banks redline the neighborhood |
| Mid-1970s | Marcus Griffith operates **Hairlox** (1315 H Street), making and selling patented hair care products |
| 1976 | **Atlas Theater closes** after 38 years; building sits vacant |
| 1985 | **H Street Community Development Corporation purchases Atlas Theater** |
| 1989 | Atlas Theater Art Moderne facade renovated |
| 1990s | Renewed interest in central city living begins slow change |
| 1993 | New group emerges to restore and reopen Atlas Theater |
| ~2000 | Jake Napier's Ultra-Modern Barbershop closes after nearly 50 years |

## Revival Era (2002-Present)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2002 | **H Street Main Street founded** in partnership with city and community members |
| 2002 | DC Office of Planning initiates community-based planning effort |
| 2002 | H Street NE Strategic Development Plan divides corridor into three districts (Urban Living, Central Retail, Arts & Entertainment) |
| May 2002 | Sprenger-Lang Foundation puts $450,000 down on Atlas Theater ($1.2 million asking price) |
| October 24, 2002 | Atlas Theater added to DC Inventory of Historic Sites |
| January 20, 2006 | DDOT announces plans for $13 million streetcar line on H Street |
| 2006 | **Atlas Performing Arts Center opens** - 59,000 square feet for area artists |
| November 10, 2010 | Atlas Theater added to National Register of Historic Places |
| 2012 | Forbes magazine names H Street **sixth-most hipster place in America** |
| 2013 | **Great American Main Street** designation from National Trust for Historic Preservation |
| February 27, 2016 | **DC Streetcar H Street/Benning line begins public service** - First streetcars in D.C. since 1962 |
| 2024 | H Street Alliance established by Councilmember Charles Allen to coordinate development strategy |
| 2024 | H Street Oral History Festival celebrates stories of long-time residents |
| March 2026 (planned) | **Streetcar service to end** - Mayor Bowser's budget plans phase-out, replacement with electric buses |

## Business and Demographic Changes

| Measure | Statistic |
|---------|-----------|
| Businesses closed since 2002 | 150+ (mostly small mom-and-pop, many Black-owned) |
| Median rent in D.C. (1980) | $443 |
| One-bedroom apartment on H Street (today) | $2,000-$3,000/month |
| Historic housing stock (pre-1939) | 66.3% of residential real estate |
| National comparison | Greater concentration than 97.9% of U.S. neighborhoods |
